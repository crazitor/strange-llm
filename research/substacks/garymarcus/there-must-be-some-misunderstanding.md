Great example last night from Denis O. on LinkedIn, that connects with pretty much everything have been writing about, from [elephants on beaches](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/wheres-waldo-the-elephant-in-the?utm_campaign=reaction&utm_medium=email&utm_source=substack&utm_content=post) to [the lack of understanding in large language models](https://open.substack.com/pub/garymarcus/p/further-trouble-in-hinton-city?r=8tdk6&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web&showWelcomeOnShare=true):

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!N2b9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fab5345a6-2f04-463d-a53b-789115a1fdb7_851x1024.jpeg)

As with everything in stochastic mimic land, this phenomenon doesn’t _always_ replicate[1](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/there-must-be-some-misunderstanding#footnote-1-141494487). But it’s not that hard to replicate, either:

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_d3R!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4fcb8436-3256-4ab7-83c7-fd30471a1c94_1170x1136.jpeg)

Examples like these highlight pretty much everything I have been saying here, from the very first post ([The New Science of Alt Intelligence](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/the-new-science-of-alt-intelligence)) to the [last](https://open.substack.com/pub/garymarcus/p/further-trouble-in-hinton-city).

  * These systems don’t deeply _understand_ their inputs, contra Dr. Hinton, [and as pointed out here regularly](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/further-trouble-in-hinton-city).

  * [Compositionality](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/three-ideas-from-linguistics-that) remains a problem. So does negation.

  * [Reliability](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/how-come-gpt-can-seem-so-brilliant) remain a problem.

  * You shouldn’t [count on these systems](https://open.substack.com/pub/garymarcus/p/could-gpt-5-revolutionize-military?r=8tdk6&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web&showWelcomeOnShare=true).

  * [Scale isn’t everything](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/the-new-science-of-alt-intelligence), and hasn’t and won’t be enough to solve any of the problems I just listed above.

  * We really [should consider trying some other approaches to AI](http://rebooting.ai).




The more things change, the more they remain the same.

Clever prompters can no doubt get results without elephants, with elaborate prompts, but so what? The question is not whether a clever user with a lot of experience and time to kill can induce a mission to do something correctly, but whether the systems _reliably_ deliver what they are asked for. They don’t.
