Google announced their big new state-of-the-art model yesterday, [Gemini 3](https://blog.google/products/gemini/gemini-3/), on most measures better than any other model out there. Still, the usual reports of silly errors and embarrassing results trickled out within hours.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LVmH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b79159e-2658-4be6-b927-5fb1eff24ede_1298x1274.png)

No surprise there. Here’s my brief hot take:

  * It’s great model, as far as LLMs go, topping most benchmarks, **but it’s certainly not AGI**. It’s haunted by the same kind of problems that all earlier models have had. Hallucinations and unreliability persist. Visual and physical reasoning are still [a mess](https://x.com/a_karvonen/status/1990839995675128259?s=61).

  * In short, **scaling isn’t getting us to AGI**. 

  * **OpenAI has basically squandered the technical lead it once had; Google has caught up.** What happens to OpenAI if Google undercuts OpenAI on price?

  * But biggest news was buried in the methods: **Google got better results than it is competitors without using Nvidia GPUs** , relying solely on their own TPUs:




[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!F2Sa!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb6de58c3-4434-43de-9a62-1c037274b103_1320x684.png)

  * **If Google were to make those TPUs commercially available at scale and reasonable price, Nvidia’s dominance would end** , price wars would begin, and compute would become a commodity. That would be huge.
