What do you think of this, I asked my eight-year-old. What is it, she asked? “A computer drew it”, I said, pointing to the caption, “Cat watching a tv with remote in hand”. 

“With a human hand??!”, she asked with suspicion, after a long pause.

MetaAI’s new text-to-movie software [Make a Video](https://makeavideo.studio/?utm_source=substack&utm_medium=email) is straight-up amazing. It’s also, as ever, stuck in the liminal space between superintelligence and super-lost. A cat holding a remote control shouldn’t have a _human_ hand. Similarly, drawing from the small number of demos on Meta’s webpage we also see this entrancing video, a “A teddy bear painting a portrait”

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9JgD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb66fc9d5-e34b-419e-941e-95dad3ab4b4c_512x512.webp)

It took me a second to notice that for all the brushwork, the painting never changed. 

Of course we see the same thing over and over in language systems, too. Earlier today I saw this lovely example from Walid Saba:

This is the uncanny valley we are in, and one that we occupy for a while.[1](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/deepfakes-versus-deep-understanding#footnote-1-76151511)

Hang on your hats.

Two mor new examples making the same point, just from today. Pranab Ghosh posted this on LinkedIn (GPT-3 in italic)

Q what should I do if my pig starts flying?

A. _You should consult your Vet_

Tim Scarfe posted the same query and got much more specific advice:

_“There is no one definitive answer to this question, as it may depend on the specific situation and on your pig's individual personality. However, some possible things you could do include trying to calm your pig down, providing it with a safe place to land, or keeping a close eye on it to make sure it doesn't injure itself."_
