On a hiking trip once, three decades ago, I accidentally coined (but failed to trademark) the phrase “self-modern, post-parody”. 

I think I have finally found a use for it. 

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!en3H!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc0df794d-8019-4fc4-b6cd-6212b66d6b5c_1377x1191.jpeg)

Want another example? Here, fully modern and way beyond parody, is Sam Altman — whose company, mind you, has never turned a profit — promising to use AI to capture the value of a large fraction of the entire universe (the earth’s l[ight cone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light_cone), to be exact).

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_cHP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2b2e87e0-eeb8-40d6-a712-e8154246e6cd_1206x1256.jpeg)

It’s almost as if the AI community _wants_ to satire itself.

And when it is not satirizing itself, big tech is regularly and predictably falling on its face, the same way, over and over again, with ever larger amounts of money. 

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yXhS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F24b425fc-810b-469f-a9b2-7fd234bd1d6a_1330x1582.png)

Funny name - [Behemoth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Behemoth) \- for absolutely massive model that so far isn’t working. Wikipedia helpfully traces the term to a “primeval chaos-monster” in the Book of Job. What could possibly go wrong?
