A few weeks ago, I wrote an essay on [ten of the challenges I expected OpenAI to face this year](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/openais-got-99-problems-and-twitch).

Things just got worse. Here’s some of what’s happened in the last two weeks.

👉 Sora demo blows minds but fails basic physics and biology

👉 One of OpenAI’s best known researchers, Andrej Karpathy, co-lead on the new agent project, departs

👉 ChatGPT has a mostly unexplained 6 hour (?) meltdown

👉 Google and Mistral close the gap w GPT-4

👉 New results from Subbarao Kambhapati cast doubts on the robustness of chain of thought prompting 

👉 Two new lawsuits again them have just been filed, one specifically on [copyright](https://news.google.com/articles/CBMiYmh0dHBzOi8vd3d3LnRoZWd1YXJkaWFuLmNvbS90ZWNobm9sb2d5LzIwMjQvZmViLzI4L21lZGlhLW91dGxldHMtc3VlLW9wZW5haS1jb3B5cmlnaHQtaW5mcmluZ2VtZW500gFiaHR0cHM6Ly9hbXAudGhlZ3VhcmRpYW4uY29tL3RlY2hub2xvZ3kvMjAyNC9mZWIvMjgvbWVkaWEtb3V0bGV0cy1zdWUtb3BlbmFpLWNvcHlyaWdodC1pbmZyaW5nZW1lbnQ?hl=en-US&gl=US&ceid=US%3Aen) and the other [a broader class action](https://www.dailydot.com/debug/chatgpt-openai-slaughterbots-lawsuit/)

👉 Microsoft starts [seeing other startups](https://www.theverge.com/2024/2/26/24083510/microsoft-mistral-partnership-deal-azure-ai)
