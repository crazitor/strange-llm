  3. They still aren’t adding a lot of quantifiable value to the world:




[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vc9J!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8c6f67d7-fc7d-4bbd-a881-42aa4f5d35df_900x1314.png)via newsletter

 _Update: This is consistent with the finding of the[ Remote Labor Index](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26787) that AI could only do about 2.5% of jobs,[ reported recently by the Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/interactive/2026/ai-jobs-automation/)._

  4. Scaling isn’t going all that well, anymore, and probably won’t cure these problems.




[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zrPY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc3f678a8-7b07-464f-8a34-de7edc9b9b1c_1547x1271.png)[Full article](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5877662)

Trying to orient our economy and geopolitical policy around such shoddy technology — particularly on the unproven hopes that it will dramatically improve– is a mistake.
