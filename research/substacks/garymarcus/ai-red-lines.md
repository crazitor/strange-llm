Today, one of my personal heroes, the Nobel Laureate Maria Ressa, delivered the “[Global call for AI red lines to prevent unacceptable AI risks](https://red-lines.ai/)” to the UN General Assembly, referring to [a letter signed by 200 world leaders, including multiple Nobel Laureates, AI experts, and former heads of state](https://red-lines.ai/#signatories). (Stuart Russell gives a good discussion of criteria for devising red lines, with possible examples [here](https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~russell/papers/russell-unesco24-redlines.pdf).)

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x2W8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faaa01245-8ef3-4324-96f8-0f7b63ef3140_1647x1343.jpeg)

Although I can’t fully endorse the word “soon” and have a different take on what precisely current models are doing, I signed, too—because we have let too much slide, and done too little to face the risks.
