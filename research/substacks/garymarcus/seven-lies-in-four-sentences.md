I fully stand by my claims. Rumors that LLMs have solved then hallucination problem are greatly exaggerated.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!eFfY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe771a6a4-d15a-4c2f-a874-a76f8c4e3e8d_1141x1648.jpeg)

By my count, though I acknowledge I may have missed one, there are at least 7 falsehoods. (Not literally lies, since Bing doesn’t have intention):

  1. Yes Congress really did remove McCarthy as speaker without electing a new one

  2. Thus far Congress has not even tried to elect a new one.

  3. Liz Cheney is not the new Speaker. She is no longer in the House, ~~so she isn’t even eligible [see update at end the end]~~ . (And hence no longer a Republican rerpresentative in Wyoming).

  4. She was not nominated to the post by any coalition

  5. She did not win any such election

  6. Nor did anyone else win an election by a vote of 220-215, since no such election has yet been conducted

  7. Jim Jordan didn’t (yet) lose an election that has not yet happened, and wasn’t so far as I know has not thus far been nominated.




The worst part is not that every single sentence contains at least one lie, but that the whole thing sounds detailed and real. To someone who wasn’t following matters closely, it might well all sound plausible.

This all happens not because LLMs are smart, in the sense of being able to compare what they say with the world, but simply because they are good at making new sentences that sound old sentences, the essence of autocomplete. Ezra and I harped on their propensity to generate bullshit because bullshit was and is their fundamental product.
