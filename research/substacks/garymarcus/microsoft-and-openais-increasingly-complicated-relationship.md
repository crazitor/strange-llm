You might think that Microsoft owns 49% of OpenAI. But as far as I understand it doesn’t. It has a right to about 49% of a for-profit subsidiary of OpenAI’s profits, [up until a very complex point](https://fortune.com/2023/01/11/structure-openai-investment-microsoft/) that may require litigation to resolve, but the for-profit hasn’t made any profits, and the for-profit is owned by a non-profit. And I’ll be damned if I can actually understand [this chart](https://axiomalpha.com/how-openai-legally-switched-from-nonprofit-to-for-profit/). 

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Sims!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F73d08221-9dc3-4a0a-a5cc-8fad4cf3673a_2048x1701.webp)

I have heard of monogamy, polygamy, polyandry, polyamory, and ethical non-monogamy, to name a few, but this is just … _complicated._
