[Here it is](https://blog.ycombinator.com/scott-aaronson-on-computational-complexity-theory-and-quantum-computers/), recorded last week at Y Combinator's office in San Francisco. For regular readers of this blog, there will be a few things that are new--research projects I've been working on this year--and many things that are old. Hope you enjoy it! Thanks so much to Craig Cannon of Y Combinator for inviting me.

Associated with the podcast, Hacker News will be doing an AMA with me later today. I'll post a link to that when it's available. [Update: here it is.](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=17425377)

I'm at [STOC'2018 TheoryFest](http://acm-stoc.org/stoc2018/) in Los Angeles right now, where theoretical computer scientists celebrated the 50th anniversary of the conference that in some sense was the birthplace of the P vs. NP problem. (Two participants in the very first STOC in 1969, Richard Karp and Allan Borodin, were on a panel to share their memories, along with Ronitt Rubinfeld and Avrim Blum, who joined the action in the 1980s.) There's been a great [program](http://acm-stoc.org/stoc2018/stocprogram.html) this year--if you'd like to ask me about it, maybe do so in the comments of this post rather than in the AMA.
