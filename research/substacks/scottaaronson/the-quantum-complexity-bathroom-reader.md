A reader named Lewis K. wrote in to ask for a "brief list of required reading for someone with a normal CS degree under his belt who wants to be taken to the research front in quantum complexity." Alright then:

[[Deutsch]](http://www.qubit.org/oldsite/resource/deutsch85.pdf) [[Bernstein-Vazirani]](http://www.cs.berkeley.edu/%7Evazirani/pubs/bv.ps) [[BBBV]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9701001) [[Simon]](http://citeseer.ist.psu.edu/simon94power.html) [[Shor]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9508027) [[Grover]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9605043) [[BBHT]](http://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9605034) [[BBCMW]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9802049) [[Ambainis]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0002066) [[Watrous]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/cs.CC/0009002) [[ANTV]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9804043) [[Fortnow-Rogers]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/cs.CC/9811023) [[Abrams-Lloyd]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9801041) [[Childs et al.]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0209131) [[DMV]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0206003) [[EHK]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0401083) [[BJK]](http://www.ee.technion.ac.il/people/zivby/papers/qcc/qccfull.ps) [[Gottesman]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9807006) [[KKR]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0406180) [[Marriott-Watrous]](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/cs.CC/0506068)

(Sprinkle in some [textbooks](http://www.amazon.com/Quantum-Computation-Information-Michael-Nielsen/dp/0521635039/sr=8-1/qid=1157570883/ref=pd_bbs_1/103-6440007-3621459?ie=UTF8&s=books), [survey](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9812037) [articles](http://www.arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0003035), and [course](http://www.cs.berkeley.edu/%7Evazirani/quantum.html) [lecture](http://theory.caltech.edu/people/preskill/ph229/) [notes](http://www.math.uwaterloo.ca/%7Eambainis/CO781.htm) to taste.)

Commenters will boil me alive for leaving out huge swaths of the field, and they'll be right. I've merely listed some papers that had a definite impact on how I, personally, attack problems. But hey, I'm the one you asked. So print 'em out, take 'em to the toilet, and sit there for a long time. When you're finished, you won't be at the "research front" -- for that you obviously have to read [my papers](http://www.scottaaronson.com/papers/) -- but hopefully you'll have seen enough to visit the [big bad arXiv](http://www.arxiv.org/list/quant-ph/new) on your own. Happy Hadamards!
