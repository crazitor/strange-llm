In which I try to give a [non-rigorous taste](http://www.scottaaronson.com/democritus/lec16.html) of the interactive proofs revolution that rocked the complexity world in the 1990's, as well as its consequences for circuit lower bounds. I argue that these results matter because they offer a tiny glimpse of how one can exploit the _structure_ of problems like 3SAT to prove lower bounds--something we know will eventually be needed for the P vs. NP question. If you got off the train before its latest tour through the Complexity Badlands, don't worry: it will double back into Philosophers' Valley (where everyone has an opinion and no one has a result) by Lecture 17 ("Fun With Anthropic Principles").
