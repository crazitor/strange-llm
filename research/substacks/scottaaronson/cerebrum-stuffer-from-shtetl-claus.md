Ho3! Home with family for the holidays and looking for something to do? Then check out the archives of our [6.893 Philosophy and Theoretical Computer Science course blog](http://philtcs.wordpress.com/). The course just ended last week, so you can find discussions of everything from the interpretation of quantum mechanics to Occam's Razor to the Church-Turing Thesis to strong AI, as well as links to student projects, including [Criticisms of the Turing Test and Why You Should Ignore (Most of) Them](http://people.csail.mit.edu/katrina/papers/6893.pdf), [Barwise Inverse Relation Principle, Bayesian Surprise, Boosting, and Other Things that Begin with the Letter B](http://people.seas.harvard.edu/~ely/ThingsThatStartWithB.pdf), and an [interactive demonstration of interactive proofs](http://web.mit.edu/~ezyang/Public/graph/svg.html). Thanks to my TA Andy Drucker, and especially to the students, for making this such an interesting course.
