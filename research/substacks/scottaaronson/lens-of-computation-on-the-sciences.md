This weekend, the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton hosted a workshop on the ["Lens of Computation in the Sciences,"](https://video.ias.edu/computationconference/2014/1122) which was organized by Avi Wigderson, and was meant to showcase theoretical computer science's imperialistic ambitions to transform every other field. I was proud to speak at the workshop, representing CS theory's designs on physics. But videos of all four of the talks are now available, and all are worth checking out:

  * [Computational Phenomena in Biology](https://video.ias.edu/computationconference/2014/1122-LeslieValiant), by Leslie Valiant
  * [Computational Phenomena in Economics](https://video.ias.edu/computationconference/2014/1122-TimRoughgarden), by Tim Roughgarden
  * [Computational Phenomena in Social Science](https://video.ias.edu/computationconference/2014/1122-JonKleinberg), by Jon Kleinberg
  * [Computational Phenomena in Physics](https://video.ias.edu/computationconference/2014/1122-ScottAaronson), by me



Unfortunately, the videos were slow to buffer when I last tried it. While you're waiting, you could also check my [PowerPoint slides](http://www.scottaaronson.com/talks/ias.ppt), though they overlap considerably with my previous talks. (As always, if you can't read PowerPoint, then go ask another reader of this blog to convert the file into a format you like.)

Thanks so much to Avi, and everyone else at IAS, for organizing an awesome workshop!
