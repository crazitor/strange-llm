On Thursday NEC put out a [press release](http://www.eurekalert.org/pub_releases/2007-05/nc-nja042707.php) announcing the "world's first controllably coupled qubits." See [here](http://www.sciencemag.org/cgi/content/abstract/316/5825/723) for the abstract of the accompanying _Science_ paper by Niskanen et al. (unfortunately the full text requires a subscription). NEC's announcement led to the usual fluffified popular articles; see [here](http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/biz/archives/2007/05/05/2003359617), [here](http://www.theregister.co.uk/2003/10/31/japanese_boffins_advance_quantum_computing/), and [here](http://www.yomiuri.co.jp/dy/features/science/20070505TDY02004.htm) for example. But to satisfy [Geordie Rose's curiosity](http://dwave.wordpress.com/2007/05/03/a-press-release-from-nec/), my hype-o-meter has _not_ yet reached D-Wave levels, for three reasons.

  1. These claims haven't garnered nearly as much 'quonfusion' as D-Wave's in the popular press.
  2. In this case there _is_ a peer-reviewed paper.
  3. There's no claim here about solving NP-complete problems, or indeed about asymptotic complexity at all. The sole claim to originality has to do with "tunable two-qubit couplings," and I'm not at all well-placed to evaluate it.



Anyway, I thought I should at least mention this work, in the hope that commenters more knowledgeable than I am will weigh in on its significance. Eternal vigilance is the price of quantum computing research.
