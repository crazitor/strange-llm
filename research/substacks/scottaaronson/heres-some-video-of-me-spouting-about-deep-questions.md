In January 2014, I attended an [FQXi conference](http://fqxi.org/conference/destination) on Vieques island in Puerto Rico. While there, [Robert Lawrence Kuhn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Lawrence_Kuhn) interviewed me for his TV program [Closer to Truth](http://www.closertotruth.com/), which deals with science and religion and philosophy and you get the idea. Alas, my interview was at the very end of the conference, and we lost track of the time--so unbeknownst to me, a plane full of theorists was literally sitting on the runway waiting for me to finish philosophizing! This was the second time Kuhn interviewed me for his show; the first time was on a cruise ship near Norway in 2011. (Thankless hero that I am, there's nowhere I won't travel for the sake of truth.)

Anyway, after a two-year wait, the videos from Puerto Rico are **[finally available online](http://closertotruth.com/contributor/scott-aaronson/profile?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=social&utm_content=new-contributor-video-promotion&utm_campaign=aaronson-tweet-2-3-2016)**. While my vignettes cover what, for most readers of this blog, will be very basic stuff, I'm _sort of_ happy with how they turned out: I still stutter and rock back and forth, _but not as much as usual_. For your viewing convenience, here are the new videos:

  * [The black hole information paradox, firewalls, and Harlow-Hayden argument](http://closertotruth.com/series/what-happens-black-holes#video-49249) (6 minutes)
  * [Physics and free will](http://closertotruth.com/series/physics-free-will#video-49247) (8 minutes 24 seconds)
  * [Which entities are conscious?](http://closertotruth.com/series/what-things-are-conscious#video-49254) (6 minutes 3 seconds)
  * [Quantum mechanics, the predictability of nature, the Bell inequality, and Einstein-certified randomness](http://closertotruth.com/series/what-cant-be-predicted-physics#video-49248) (5 minutes 12 seconds)
  * [What's the value of philosophy, and can it make progress?](http://closertotruth.com/series/why-philosophy-science#video-49255) (3 minutes 42 seconds)
  * [Newcomb's Paradox](http://closertotruth.com/series/what-causation#video-49250) (4 minutes 13 seconds)
  * [Gödel's Theorem and the definiteness of mathematical truth](http://closertotruth.com/series/what-truth#video-49253) (8 minutes 20 seconds)



I had one other vignette, about why the universe exists, but they seem to have cut that one. Alas, if I knew why the universe existed in January 2014, I can't remember any more.

One embarrassing goof: I referred to the inventor of Newcomb's Paradox as "Simon Newcomb." Actually it was [William Newcomb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Newcomb): a distant relative of [Simon Newcomb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simon_Newcomb), the 19th-century astronomer who measured the speed of light.
