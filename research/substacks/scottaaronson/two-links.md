1\. The CRA's [Computing Community Consortium](http://www.cra.org/ccc/), chaired by national treasure [Ed Lasowska](http://lazowska.cs.washington.edu/) of the University of Washington, recently put up a website with [fifteen brief essays](http://www.cra.org/ccc/initiatives) about "Computing Research Initiatives for the 21st Century." These essays will apparently be reviewed by the science policy staff at the Obama transition office. Dave Bacon and I wrote the [essay on quantum computing](http://www.cra.org/ccc/docs/init/Quantum_Computing.pdf)--or rather, Dave wrote it with his inimitable enthusiasm, and then I tried in vain to moderate it slightly. (When Dave told me that President-elect Obama needed my help with quantum computing policy, what was I going to say? "Sorry, I'm doing my laundry this weekend"?)

2\. Lee Gomes of _Forbes_ magazine [wrote a fun article](http://www.forbes.com/home/2008/12/10/hot-topics-contradictions-tech-sciences_cz_lg_1211gomes.html) about the Worldview Manager project that I [blogged about a while ago](https://scottaaronson.blog/?p=362). (For some reason, Lee wasn't deterred by my pointing out to him that the project hasn't even started yet.)
