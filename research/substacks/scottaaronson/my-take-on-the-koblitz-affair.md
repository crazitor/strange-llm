Now that [Luca](http://in-theory.blogspot.com/2007/08/swift-boating-of-modern-cryptography.html), [Michael Mitzenmacher](http://mybiasedcoin.blogspot.com/2007/08/how-mathematicians-view-computer.html), [Jonathan Katz](http://weblog.fortnow.com/2007/08/koblitz-controversy-reaction.html), and [Oded Goldreich](http://www.wisdom.weizmann.ac.il/~oded/X/pmc-ltr.txt) have all weighed in on Neal Koblitz's [critique of modern cryptography](http://www.ams.org/notices/200708/tx070800972p.pdf) in the _Notices of the AMS_ , I can no longer bear to be left out of the action.

My reaction is simple: we computer scientists should feel _honored_ that the mathematicians have finally bestowed on us the level of contempt they once reserved for the physicists.

**Update (9/6):** If you want to understand what's _actually_ involved in this controversy, the best starting point I've found is [this paper](http://www.springerlink.com/content/7252778202hl3086/fulltext.pdf) by Ivan Damgård.
