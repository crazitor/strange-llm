Yesterday brought the tragic news that [Mihai Pătraşcu](http://people.csail.mit.edu/mip/)--who revolutionized the field of data structures since he burst onto the scene a decade ago--has passed away at the age of 29, after a year-and-a-half-long battle with brain cancer. Mihai was not only an outstanding researcher but a fun-loving, larger-than-life personality in the computer science theory community. For more information, see [Lance and Bill's](http://blog.computationalcomplexity.org/2012/06/mihai-patrascu-1982-2012.html) or [Michael Mitzenmacher's](http://mybiasedcoin.blogspot.com/2012/06/sad-passing-mihai-patrascu.html) blogs.

Mihai was an MIT CS PhD student (advised by Erik Demaine), who worked on the same floor as me for the first couple years I was here. I'm still in shock over his loss--I hadn't even known about the cancer before yesterday. Mihai and I had pretty big disagreements, mostly over the viability of quantum computing, the "technical" versus "conceptual" theory debate, various things he wrote on [his blog](http://infoweekly.blogspot.com/) and various things I wrote on mine. But it seems terribly stupid now to have let this stuff get in the way of collegiality. I feel guilty for not trying to mend bridges with him when I had the chance.

Rest in peace, Mihai.
