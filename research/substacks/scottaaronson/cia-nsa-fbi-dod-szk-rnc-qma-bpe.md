Sorry for the long delay! I had to be in Washington D.C. this week, for reasons I'm not at liberty to disclose. (Yes, I'm serious, and no, it's not as interesting as it sounds.) Oh: on my way back to Canada, for some strange reason they confiscated my Blistex. I guess airport security guards get chapped lips a lot.

As our world descends even further into war, terror, and Armageddon, I have an exciting complexity-theoretic announcement. Building on the [Complexity Zoo](http://www.complexityzoo.com), Greg Kuperberg has created a "Robozoologist": an expert system for reasoning about complexity classes. What's more, Greg is releasing some [spinoffs](http://www.math.ucdavis.edu/%7Egreg/zoology/) of his project to the masses, including a [JavaScript-powered inclusion graph](http://www.math.ucdavis.edu/%7Egreg/zoology/diagram.xml), and an automatically-generated [RoboZoo](http://www.math.ucdavis.edu/%7Egreg/zoology/relations.html). I can still remember them frontier days of 2002, when I had to herd the BP operators with my two bare hands…
