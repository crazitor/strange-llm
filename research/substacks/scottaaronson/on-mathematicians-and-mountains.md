[Luca](http://lucatrevisan.wordpress.com/2008/09/03/oded-schramm/) and [Terry Tao](http://terrytao.wordpress.com/2008/09/03/oded-schramm/) have already reported the [tragic loss](http://research.microsoft.com/schramm/) of the brilliant probabilist [Oded Schramm](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oded_Schramm) in a hiking accident. I didn't know Oded, but I knew some of his great results and was deeply saddened by the news. My heartfelt condolences go out to his friends and family.

It was [two years ago](https://scottaaronson.blog/?p=121) that we lost [Misha Alekhnovich](http://www.math.ias.edu/~misha/), who I did know, in a whitewater rafting accident. Other mathematicians and scientists lost in similar ways have included [Heinz Pagels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinz_Pagels), [Jacques Herbrand](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Herbrand), [Raymond Paley](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raymond_Paley), [Krzysztof Galicki](http://www.abqtrib.com/news/2007/sep/26/unm-professor-galicki-who-loved-math-and-hiking-de/), and [Erik Rauch](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erik_Rauch). The teenage Einstein very nearly died while hiking on a mountain near Zurich. I have more than one irreplaceable colleague who's repeatedly courted death on the ski slopes.

I'd like to issue a plea to any mathematicians and scientists who might be reading: _please go easier on the extreme outdoor activities._ Let those who live for such things demonstrate their daring by gambling their lives; those who live for the ages can find safer recreations. The world needs more nerds, not fewer.
