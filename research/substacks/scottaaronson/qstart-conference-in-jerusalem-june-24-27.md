Friend-of-the-blog Dorit Aharonov asked me to advertise the [QStart Conference](http://www.as.huji.ac.il/q-start), which will be held at Hebrew University of Jerusalem June 24-27 of this year, to celebrate the opening of Hebrew University's new [Quantum Information Science Center](http://qcent.huji.ac.il/). Speakers include Yakir Aharonov, Jacob Bekenstein, Hans Briegel, Ed Farhi, Patrick Hayden, Ray Laflamme, Elon Lindenstrauss, Alex Lubotzky, John Martinis, Barbara Terhal, Umesh Vazirani, Stephanie Wehner, Andrew Yao … and me, your humble blogger (who will actually be there with Lily, on her first trip abroad--or for that matter, beyond the Boston metropolitan area). Dorit tells me that the conference should be of interest to mathematicians, physicists, chemists, philosophers, and computer scientists; that registration is open now; and that student travel support is available. Oh, and if you're one of the people who think quantum computing is bunk? As displayed on the poster above, leading QC skeptic Gil Kalai is a **co-organizer** of the conference.
