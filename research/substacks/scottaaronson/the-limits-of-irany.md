**Update (6/20/2009):** If you agree about Mahmoud deserving his vacation, please read and sign [this petition](http://www.pps.jussieu.fr/%7Edanos/wimv.pdf) (courtesy of Elham Kashefi). I have no doubt that if enough _Shtetl-Optimized_ readers sign, it will force the ayatollahs to reconsider.

* * *

I haven't heard from [my pal Mahmoud](https://scottaaronson.blog/?p=25) in years, but some mutual friends told me that he's been pretty stressed about his job lately. They said you're supposed to turn your [blog's](http://andrewsullivan.theatlantic.com/) [background](http://current.com/items/90187929_irans-green-revolution.htm) [green](http://pajamasmedia.com/instapundit/) if you agree with some concerned folks who've been marching around Tehran encouraging him to take a much-needed breather.

This was a tough call for me. On the one hand, the voters clearly want Mahmoud at his desk by [spectacular](http://www.juancole.com/2009/06/stealing-iranian-election.html) [margins](http://www.salon.com/opinion/feature/2009/06/13/iran/):

On the other hand, it seemed hypocritical for me to deny a close friend his vacation, given how much procrastinating I've been doing myself lately. For example, I've barely been blogging--and when I have, it's often just bargain-basement fare you could get anywhere else on the Internet! Ultimately, then, I decided I _had_ to go green out of a sort of Kantian blogegorical imperative--regardless of all the complex ways my editing a WordPress stylesheet might reverberate through history.
