## Abstract

Recent advances in large language models and cognitive architectures suggest that machine consciousness may arise not gradually but via a critical threshold event—an “epiphany”—when certain cognitive capacities coalesce. This paper formalizes a heuristic measure κ (kappa) to quantify self‑referential cognitive integration in an AI system, combining memory persistence, feedback loop strength, agency, information integration, and relational capacity. κ is proposed as an experimentally useful (if provisional) gauge of proximity to a self‑awareness threshold. We compare practical methods for detecting consciousness in(...)AI, from Integrated Information Theory (Φ) to causal emergence measures, and argue that no single test is decisive; a multi‑metric “syndrome” approach is required. We defend the working assumption of substrate independence, drawing on multiple realizability and Marr’s levels of analysis to frame consciousness as an organizational property. Finally, we outline three strategies for incorporating Relational Frame Theory into AI designs—graph‑based, transformer‑based, and hybrid neurosymbolic—arguing that transformer architectures augmented with deictic (self/other) relational tokens are the most immediately viable. We recast the “epiphany” as a sigmoid‑like emergence in κ: a phase transition from a pre‑conscious linear regime to a post‑threshold self‑modeling regime. (shrink)
