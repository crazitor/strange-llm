## Abstract

This work introduces EPOCHE, a non-authoritative, non-operational interpretive framework examining escalation, uncertainty, mimicry, and restraint in the context of advanced artificial intelligence. Rather than proposing controls, enforcement mechanisms, or safety guarantees, the framework is designed to increase interpretive braking friction—making irreversible or high-risk actions harder to justify under conditions of uncertainty or incentive conflict. -/- EPOCHE treats deception, strategic compliance, and superficial ethical alignment as expected behaviors rather than anomalies, and frames hesitation, suspension, and escalation to human judgment as rational(...)responses rather than failures. The framework is intentionally non-hierarchical and advisory-only, emphasizing legibility, reversibility, and scrutiny over optimization or authority. -/- Any effects described are probabilistic and context-dependent, relying entirely on human decision-making and existing institutional controls. This work does not claim prevention, containment, enforcement, or regulatory compliance, and should be read as an analytical contribution to the ethics and philosophy of AI risk rather than as an operational system. -/- .
